## Bab 1: Buying Some Mangoes and Spinach
**Topik utama:** fruits, vegetables, grocery products  
**Sub-konsep:**
- Two kilos of mangoes
- A bunch of spinach
- Countable and uncountable nouns
- Asking and answering about quantity and price
**Teks bacaan:** |
  Surya and the Tasty Porridge  
  Adapted from Goldilocks and the Three Bears  
  
  There was a little boy named Surya. He went for a walk in the forest and found a house of three big bears. The bears welcomed him to their house.  
  
  In the kitchen, Surya saw three bowls of porridge. He tasted the first one, but it was too salty. The second one was too sweet. The third one was too sour.  
  “Oh no!” said Surya.  
  
  Surya saw colorful vegetables in the kitchen; carrots, spinach, and cabbage. “I could make the porridge tasty,” said Surya to the three bears.  
  
  In the first bowl, Surya added three long carrots. The bears counted, “One, two, three long carrots!”  
  
  In the second bowl, he added five leaves of green spinach. The bears counted, “One, two, three, four, five leaves of green spinach!”  
  
  In the third bowl, he added three pieces of white cabbage. The bears counted, “One, two, three pieces of white cabbage!”  
  
  Surya tasted the porridge again. “Now, the porridge is delicious!” Surya exclaimed.  
  
  The three bears could finally have their tasty porridge. Hooray!  
  
  But, what about Surya? Was he having a bowl of tasty porridge?
**Kosakata kunci:** fruits, vegetables, grocery products, mangoes, spinach, countable, uncountable, kitchen  
**Kompetensi yang diuji:** siswa dapat membuat percakapan pendek tentang buah, menanyakan dan menjawab jumlah/harga, menyebutkan buah dan sayur, membedakan countable dan uncountable nouns, serta menulis kalimat sederhana tentang bahan belanjaan.

## Bab 2: In the Kitchen
**Topik utama:** kitchen utensils, positions of objects, cooking steps, making a special recipe
**Sub-konsep:**
- kitchen utensils and their positions
- asking for and giving directions to objects in the kitchen
- cooking actions: cut, heat, spread, toast, place, put, add
- recipe ingredients and steps
- sequencing cooking steps
**Teks bacaan:** |
  A recipe for a special sandwich

  Ingredients:
  One slice of fried tempeh
  Two slices of bread
  Two slices of tomato
  Two slices of cucumber
  One tablespoon of margarine
  Some ketchup

  Steps:
  1. Spread margarine on the bread.
  2. Heat some margarine in a pan.
  3. Toast the bread until golden brown.
  4. Place a slice of bread on a plate.
  5. Put tempeh, tomato, and cucumber on the bread.
  6. Add the ketchup.
  7. Put another slice of bread on top.
  8. Cut the sandwich in half.

  Now, the special sandwich is ready to eat.

  Have you heard of a hotdog? We are going to make a recipe for a banana hotdog.

  Ingredients:
  One banana fritter
  One tablespoon of margarine
  Some sprinkles
  Chocolate jam
  One hot dog bun

  A banana hot dog

  Steps:
  Cut the hot dog bun in half.
  Spread margarine on the bread.
  Heat some margarine in a pan.
  Toast the bun until golden brown.
  Place the bun on a plate.
  Put the banana fritter into the hot dog bun.
  Add the chocolate jam on the banana fritter.
  Add some sprinkles on top of the banana fritter.

  Now, the banana hot dog is ready to eat.
**Kosakata kunci:** kitchen utensils, spread, toast, heat, place, cut, ingredients, recipe
**Kompetensi yang diuji:** siswa mampu menyebutkan peralatan dapur, menjelaskan posisi benda, memahami dan mengurutkan langkah memasak, serta melengkapi resep sederhana

## Bab 3: Visiting Bara’s House
**Topik utama:** directions, neighbourhood, places in the neighbourhood, prepositions of place
**Sub-konsep:**
- asking and giving directions
- naming public places in a neighbourhood
- describing locations with in front of, beside, behind, between
- understanding a story about visiting a friend
- drawing and presenting a neighbourhood map
**Teks bacaan:** |
  Visiting Bara

  Bara was unwell. Pipit, Mita and Ferdinand are visiting him.

  Pipit, Mita, Ferdinand, and Bara love playing games together.

  One Saturday afternoon, Pipit suggested playing hide and seek in the playground. “Let’s hide in fun places!” she exclaimed.

  Ferdinand closed his eyes and counted near the big tree.

  Pipit, Mita, and Bara ran to find hiding spots. Pipit and Mita hid behind a bench beside the school. Bara found a spot behind the bushes next to the mosque. They waited quietly for Ferdinand to find them.

  Ferdinand finished counting and began searching. He looked around and found Pipit and Mita giggling behind the bench. “Found you!” he shouted.

  Now it was Pipit’s turn to seek. Mita hid between the trees, while Ferdinand hid under the slide. Bara hid behind the bushes next to the mosque. Pipit searched high and low until she found Mita between the trees.

  With big smiles, they decided it was Mita’s turn to seek. But it began to rain. They quickly ran and gathered in front of the grocery store.

  They waited for the rain to stop, but Bara ran and played in the rain. Pipit, Mita, and Ferdinand followed him to the playground.

  Splish, Splash, Splosh! They jumped in the muddy puddle. They got all wet and dirty but were very happy.
**Kosakata kunci:** directions, neighbourhood, market, beach, beside, behind, between, under
**Kompetensi yang diuji:** siswa mampu meminta dan memberi petunjuk arah, mengenali tempat di lingkungan sekitar, menggunakan preposisi tempat dengan tepat, dan memahami urutan cerita sederhana

## Bab 5: At the Library
**Topik utama:** library rules, borrowing books, asking politely, book review
**Sub-konsep:**
- aturan perpustakaan
- meminta izin secara sopan
- meminjam buku
- memberi pendapat tentang buku
- menulis ulasan buku
**Teks bacaan:** |
  Bara, Pipit, Mita and Ferdinand are at the library. They want to borrow some books.

  It’s Bara’s birthday and the students borrowed some books from the library. They really liked the books. But today, they must return them.

  “I really like the story in ‘Beauty and the Beast,’” said Mita. “The princess character in the story likes reading, and the Beast character has a big library. I will give it five stars.” “Do you like your book Pipit?” asked Mita.

  “I really like the book. I think I can give it four stars,” said Pipit. “Why not five stars?” asked Bara. “I like the book, but I think it should have more pictures. I want to see a picture of the Taj Mahal,” answered Pipit. “Do you like your book, Bara?” asked Pipit.

  “The comic is very funny! I love it! I will give it five stars,” said Bara. “Really? Can you tell us more?” asked Ferdinand. “So, the story was about dinosaurs that got lost in a city. But they finally found a way back to their world. The main character’s name is Dinky Dino. It’s very silly,” Bara told the story while laughing.

  Then Ferdinand said, “I don’t really like the book How to Play Chess, it’s really boring. I don’t understand the explanations. I will give it one star. Let’s return the books.”

  “Hey, look at the sign here. It says, ‘Book Return’. I think we can put our books here,” said Pipit. Mita, Pipit, Bara and Ferdinand put their books on the table. Then, they left the library.

  The book contains science facts about tropical fruits like starfruit, pineapples, and bananas. These fruits only grow in tropical countries such as Malaysia, Indonesia, and Thailand. They are popular because they are fresh and juicy.
**Kosakata kunci:** library, borrow, return, polite, review
**Kompetensi yang diuji:** siswa mampu memahami aturan perpustakaan, meminta sesuatu dengan sopan, mengenali isi buku, dan menyampaikan pendapat atau ulasan sederhana tentang buku

## Bab 6: Reading a Lot of Stories

**Sub-konsep:**

- Listen and point

- Game and role play activities
- Rewrite endings and stories using character options

  **The Mouse Deer and the Crocodiles**

  Once upon a time, a hungry mouse deer was in the forest. He went to the river looking for food.

  When he arrived there, he saw a lot of fruit across the river.

  But, he also saw so many crocodiles in the river. He came up with a brilliant idea! He called up the crocodiles, “Hi, crocodiles!”

  “Hi, mouse deer, you come in time for our lunch!” said the crocodiles.

  “I know that you are hungry. But look at you, there are so many of you and only one me,” answered the mouse deer.

  “Don’t worry. I’ll make sure that each of you will have me equally. But don’t eat me yet. I will jump and count on you,” said the crocodile.

  The mouse deer jumped onto the crocodile’s back and counted “one, two, three ...”

  He kept jumping and counting until he reached back to the other side of the river.

  The mouse deer ran quickly to the fruits.

  **The Boy Who Cried Wolf**

  Once upon a time, there was a shepherd boy named Tomi. He looked after his sheep on the hill.

  The boy loved to have fun. He sometimes played pranks.

  One day, the boy had an idea for a prank. He shouted, “Wolves! wolves! The wolves are coming to eat my sheep.”

  The villagers ran to the hill. They were worried about the boy. However, there was no wolf.

  The boy laughed. He was very happy with the prank.

  The next day, the boy did the same prank. He shouted, “Wolves! Wolves! The wolves are coming to eat my sheep.”

  The villagers ran again to the hill.

  “There’s no wolf. It was just another prank!” said the boy. He felt very pleased.

  The villagers became disappointed in the boy.

  Two days later, when the boy looked after his sheep, he saw wolves. He shouted, “Wolves! Wolves! Help!”. The boy was scared.

  But this time, the villagers did not come.

**Kosakata kunci:** mouse deer, crocodiles, pranks, shepherd, worried, disappointed
**Kompetensi yang diuji:** siswa mampu memahami urutan cerita, mengenali past tense verbs, memahami tokoh dan alur cerita imajinatif, menjawab pertanyaan isi cerita, serta memerankan dan menulis ulang cerita dengan benar
